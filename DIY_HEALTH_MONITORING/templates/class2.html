Fever Diagnosis & Management

Comprehensive guide to understanding, treating, and recovering from fever

Common Causes of Fever

Viral Infections
Respiratory viruses (common cold, influenza), gastrointestinal viruses, and other viral illnesses are the most common causes of fever. These infections trigger the immune system to raise body temperature as a defense mechanism.
Bacterial Infections
Strep throat, ear infections, urinary tract infections, pneumonia, and other bacterial infections can cause significant fever. Bacterial fevers often require medical attention and antibiotic treatment.
Ear Infections (Otitis Media)
Middle ear infections cause inflammation and fluid buildup, leading to fever along with symptoms like ear pain, discharge, and hearing difficulties. Common in children but can affect adults.
Dehydration
Insufficient fluid intake can cause mild fever as the body struggles to regulate temperature. This is particularly common in children, elderly individuals, and during hot weather.

Treatment & Recovery Timeline

First 24 Hours
Focus on rest and hydration. Use lukewarm compresses and over-the-counter fever reducers if needed. Monitor temperature every 4-6 hours.
Days 2-3
Continue hydration and rest. Introduce light, nutritious foods. If fever persists above 38.5°C, consult healthcare provider for potential infection assessment.
Days 4-7
Fever should gradually subside. Gradually return to normal activities while maintaining good nutrition and hydration. Complete any prescribed medication courses.

⚠️ When to Seek Immediate Medical Attention

  • Fever above 40°C (104°F) that doesn't respond to medication
  • Fever lasting more than 3 days
  • Severe headache, stiff neck, or confusion
  • Difficulty breathing or chest pain
  • Persistent vomiting or inability to keep fluids down
  • Seizures or convulsions
  • Skin rash that doesn't fade when pressed

Treatment Recommendations

Rest & Hydration
Get plenty of rest to allow your body to fight the infection. Drink at least 8-10 glasses of water daily, plus electrolyte solutions if needed. Avoid caffeine and alcohol as they can cause dehydration.
Temperature Management
Use lukewarm sponge baths or cool compresses on forehead, wrists, and neck. Wear lightweight, breathable clothing. Keep room temperature comfortable (20-22°C).
Medication Guidance
Use acetaminophen or ibuprofen as directed for fever reduction. Never give aspirin to children or teenagers. Complete antibiotic courses if prescribed for bacterial infections.

Optimal Diet Plan for Fever Recovery

Fruits & Vegetables
Rich in vitamins and antioxidants to support immune function
Lean Proteins
Chicken, fish, eggs for tissue repair and immune support
Whole Grains
Oats, brown rice for sustained energy and fiber
Hydration
Water, herbal teas, broths to prevent dehydration
Recommended Foods
Eat: Chicken soup, vegetable broths, bananas, applesauce, toast, rice, oatmeal, steamed vegetables, lean proteins. These foods are easy to digest and provide essential nutrients.

Avoid: Processed foods, fried items, sugary drinks, dairy products (if producing mucus), spicy foods, and caffeine. These can worsen dehydration or digestive discomfort.

Prevention Strategies

Hygiene Practices
Wash hands frequently with soap and water, use alcohol-based sanitizers, and avoid touching face with unwashed hands.
Vaccination
Stay updated with recommended vaccinations including annual flu shots to prevent common fever-causing illnesses.
